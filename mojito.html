Mojito

A rum -based cocktail, its name comes from the Spanish word 'Mojo', meaning magic amulet . Basically, it can be said that mint is added to rum fizz , but the refreshing taste of mint gives it a much fresher taste. The bright green color of lime and mint stands out, giving a sense of refreshment visually. The taste is sweet, with a hint of mint and a bitter aftertaste.

Ingredients 1 1/2 oz rum 2/3 oz fresh lime juice 6ea mint leaves 2 Tsp (about 10g) Tablet back sugar fillup sparkling water
How to make 1. Place mint leaves and 1 lime wedge into a sturdy glass.
Use a muddler and crush to release mint oils and lime juice. 2. Add remaining lime wedges and 2 tablespoons sugar, and muddle again to release the lime juice.
Do not strain the mixture. 3. Fill the glass almost to the top with ice. 4. Pour in rum and fill the glass with club soda. 5. Stir, taste, and add more sugar if desired.
