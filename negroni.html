Negroni

The negroni is a cocktail, made of equal parts gin, vermouth rosso (red, semi-sweet), and Campari, generally served on the rocks, and commonly garnished with an orange slice or orange peel. The basic recipe – an equal-parts cocktail of these three ingredients – is first recorded in French cocktail books of the late 1920s, alongside many similar drinks; in Italy a long drink of equal parts vermouth and Campari (but no gin), topped with soda and served over ice, has existed since the 1800s under the names Milano–Torino or Americano. There are claims of Italian drinks by the name "Negroni" containing gin from 1919, though these differ significantly from the modern drink

Ingredients 1 oz gin 1 oz sweet red vermouth 1 oz campari Ice
How to make 1. Add the gin, Campari and sweet vermouth to a mixing glass filled with ice, and stir until well-chilled. 2. Strain into a rocks glass over a large ice cube. 3. Garnish with an orange peel.
